For decades, Artificial Intelligence (AI) was a protagonist of science fiction—a distant promise of gleaming cities and sentient machines. However, as we stand in 2026, the narrative has shifted from "what if" to "how much." AI is no longer a localized tool or a chatbot on a website; it has become the "electricity" of the 21st century—a foundational layer of infrastructure that powers global economies, medical breakthroughs, and the very fabric of our social interactions.
